TOPIC: Points Redemption Strategy Guide
CATEGORY: Strategy

VALUATION_BENCHMARKS:
Understanding cents per point (cpp) is essential for smart redemptions.
- Below 1.0 cpp = Poor value. Avoid these redemptions.
- 1.0 to 1.5 cpp = Below average. Only use if no better option exists.
- 1.5 to 2.0 cpp = Average to good. Acceptable for most redemptions.
- 2.0 to 2.5 cpp = Good value. This is the target for hotel transfers.
- Above 2.5 cpp = Excellent value. Sweet spot redemptions.

SPLIT_PAYMENT_STRATEGY:
When a hotel stay costs more than your points cover, split payment
(points + cash) is often the right move. Key rules:
- Only use points if you get at least 1.5 cents per point in value.
- If the cash remainder is small (under $50), points redemption is worth it.
- If the cash remainder exceeds the hotel cost by more than 50%, reconsider.
- Never redeem points for less than 1.0 cent per point — just pay cash.

TRANSFER_TIMING:
- Transfer points only when you have a confirmed booking in mind.
- Points transferred to hotel programs cannot be transferred back.
- Always verify hotel availability BEFORE transferring points.
- Transfer ratios are set by the credit card company and change rarely,
  but always confirm the current ratio before a large transfer.

DENOMINATION_RULES:
Credit card companies require transfers in fixed increments.
Common increments: 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 / 25,000 / 50,000 points.
You cannot transfer 4,500 or 12,000 points — only exact increments.
Always transfer the minimum needed to cover the stay, not more.
Unused hotel points expire faster than credit card points.

HOTEL_PROGRAM_OVERVIEW:
- Marriott Bonvoy: Largest hotel network. Points worth ~0.8 cents each.
  Best for mid-tier properties. Requires more points per night than Hyatt.
- World of Hyatt: Smaller network but highest point value at 1.5-2.5 cpp.
  Best program for luxury redemptions. Limited properties.
- Hilton Honors: Largest hotel count globally. Points worth ~0.5 cents.
  Requires many points per night but has wide availability.
- IHG One Rewards: Best for budget and mid-tier stays. Fourth night free
  benefit makes multi-night bookings very efficient.

WHICH_CARD_FOR_WHICH_HOTEL:
- Marriott Bonvoy: Best = Chase Sapphire Preferred (1:1, 2.0 cpp source)
- World of Hyatt: Best = Chase Sapphire Preferred (1:1, highest sweet spots)
- Hilton Honors: Best = Amex Gold (2:1 ratio, doubles your points)
- IHG One Rewards: Best = Chase Sapphire Preferred or Capital One Venture X

MULTIPLE_CARD_STRATEGY:
When a user has multiple cards, always compare:
1. Transfer ratio to the target hotel program
2. Source points valuation (cpp)
3. Current balance vs minimum denomination
4. Which card has the most points to spare
Never deplete a card's points entirely — keep a buffer for future use.
