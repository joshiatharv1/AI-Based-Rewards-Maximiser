CARD: American Express Gold Card
ISSUER: American Express
POINTS_CURRENCY: American Express Membership Rewards
VALUATION: 2.2 cents per point
ANNUAL_FEE: $250

HOTEL_PARTNERS:
- Marriott Bonvoy | Transfer ratio 1:0.8 | Minimum 5,000 points
- Hilton Honors | Transfer ratio 1:2.0 | Minimum 1,000 points

BEST_USE:
American Express Gold is best used for Hilton Honors transfers.
The 2:1 transfer ratio means 1,000 Amex MR becomes 2,000 Hilton points.
Hilton Honors points are worth about 0.5 cents each, so 25,000 Amex MR
becomes 50,000 Hilton points worth approximately $250 — matching the
1.0 cent per Amex point baseline, which is acceptable.

For Marriott Bonvoy, the 0.8x transfer ratio is a disadvantage.
1,000 Amex MR becomes only 800 Marriott points. Marriott points
are worth 0.8 cents each, so the effective value is about 0.64 cents
per Amex MR point — below the 2.2 cent valuation. Avoid Marriott
transfers with Amex Gold unless no other card is available.

AVOID: Transferring Amex MR to Marriott Bonvoy. The 0.8x ratio
combined with lower Marriott point values creates poor overall value.
Chase or Citi cards are better for Marriott redemptions.

SWEET_SPOT: Hilton luxury properties during off-peak periods.
25,000 Amex MR = 50,000 Hilton points, enough for 1-2 nights
at a mid-tier Hilton, delivering solid value for the transfer.
